Linda from Convometrics here. We've analyzed 146k+ AI conversations, including 25k+ roleplay sessions, and discovered something counterintuitive about character engagement.

**The paradox:** Users with the highest message counts often have the lowest actual satisfaction scores. They're not more engaged—they're stuck in loops trying to get the character to behave correctly.

**What we found in roleplay conversations:**
- **Character Break Detection**: 34% of long sessions include character inconsistency that users try to correct with multiple prompts
- **The Politeness Trap**: Users say "that's perfect, thanks" even when the character completely missed their intent  
- **Engagement vs Satisfaction**: 15+ message sessions have 23% lower quality scores than 5-7 message sessions
- **Abandonment Signals**: Specific phrases users write before giving up on a character entirely

**Character.AI-specific insights:**
- Romantic roleplay has higher false satisfaction than adventure/storytelling
- Users retry the same character interaction 3.2x on average before switching characters
- "Depth" conversations (character consistency, emotional intelligence) retain users 5x longer than "breadth" conversations

**The business impact:** Traditional metrics show high engagement = success. Reality shows high engagement often = user frustration with character quality.
