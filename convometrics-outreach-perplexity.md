I'm reaching out because I discovered something in our analysis of 146K+ AI conversations that could be relevant to search-based AI: **the difference between factually correct answers and actually helpful ones.**

**Pattern we're seeing:**
1. User asks: "How do I fix a leaky faucet?"  
2. AI provides comprehensive, factually accurate answer
3. User says "thanks" and closes the session
4. System logs: ✅ Query resolved
5. Reality: User still couldn't fix their faucet and tried Google 30 minutes later

**Why this might matter for Perplexity:**
- **19%** of users who gave positive feedback ("thanks," "helpful") came back within 1 hour with the same or related query
- Users who get "perfect" answers but still search elsewhere rate the experience 2.8/5 vs 4.2/5 for truly resolved queries
- Multi-turn follow-ups often reveal the initial answer missed the user's actual context/skill level

**What we found works:**
- **Context gap detection**: Identifying when answers assume knowledge the user doesn't have
- **Completion prediction**: Distinguishing "polite closure" from "actually satisfied"  
- **Intent depth analysis**: Understanding when "how to X" really means "help me troubleshoot X"

**For Perplexity:** This could help you identify which answer patterns drive genuine resolution vs. search abandonment, and optimize for actual user success rather than just factual accuracy.
